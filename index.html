DARPA · 2004–2007

The Grand Challenge

Prize Competitions, Field Creation, and the Challenge Model

Between 2004 and 2007, DARPA ran three consecutive prize competitions that seeded Waymo, Aurora, Nuro, and Zoox — with under $7M in prize money. The story is not about autonomous vehicles. It is about what a well-designed challenge can do that a traditional funded program cannot: shift community belief, attract outside talent, and create the conditions for a field to take itself seriously.

DARPA BTO · 2022–2026

The Triage Challenge

Designing a Grand Challenge for a Data-Scarce, Life-Critical Domain

The DARPA Triage Challenge is a three-year open competition attempting to do for mass casualty triage what the Grand Challenge did for autonomous vehicles. Designed in part by BiTS co-director JP Chretien, it illustrates how to build challenge infrastructure — including data collection programs (RITMO) — before competition launch in domains where the usual prerequisites don't yet exist.

About BiTS

The Big if True Science Accelerator is a 15-week, part-time program run by Renaissance Philanthropy that trains scientists and engineers to design and lead high-impact coordinated research programs at organisations like DARPA, ARPA-E, ARIA, ARPA-H, and SPRIND.

About this resource

This site collects the BiTS curriculum playbook and case study library. Content is maintained in the bits-renphil/content GitHub repository. Curriculum chapters cover the full arc of program management; case studies extract design lessons for the SPRIND challenge model.

Who is this for?

Current and aspiring program managers at mission-driven research agencies and philanthropies — including DARPA, ARPA-E, ARIA, ARPA-H, SPRIND, JEDI, and Focused Research Organizations — who want practical frameworks for designing programs that work.
